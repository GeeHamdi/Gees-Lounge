About the Book

What happens when the people who should feel safest do not?

Families shape the first stories we learn about love, loyalty, responsibility, identity, and belonging. But not every family teaches those lessons gently.

Welcome to My Toxic Family explores the roles people are pushed into, the behaviours families normalise, and the emotional rules that can keep harmful patterns alive across generations.

The book examines guilt, favouritism, silence, control, emotional blackmail, scapegoating, parentification, and the painful confusion that appears when love and harm exist in the same relationship.

Inside the Book

Key themes

01

Family Roles

How people become the favourite, the scapegoat, the fixer, the invisible child, or the one expected to carry everyone else.

02

Emotional Manipulation

How guilt, obligation, fear, silence, comparison, and conditional affection can be used to control family members.

03

Inherited Patterns

Why unhealthy behaviours can pass from one generation to another and become mistaken for normal family life.

04

Boundaries

Why setting limits can feel like betrayal in a family that expects obedience, silence, or constant emotional access.

05

Loyalty & Guilt

The conflict between protecting yourself and feeling responsible for maintaining the family at any personal cost.

06

Reclaiming Identity

Learning to separate who you are from the role your family assigned to you—and choosing what comes next.

For Reflective Readers

Who should read it?

✓ Readers trying to understand difficult, controlling, or emotionally exhausting family relationships.

✓ Anyone who has been assigned a fixed role within the family and struggles to step outside it.

✓ People learning to recognise guilt, manipulation, favouritism, emotional blackmail, and unhealthy loyalty.

✓ Readers who want clarity without simplistic advice about forgiving, cutting people off, or pretending everything is fine.

Why I wrote it

I wrote Welcome to My Toxic Family because family harm is often dismissed, minimised, or defended simply because it comes from people connected to us by blood.

This book is about recognising patterns clearly, understanding how they shape identity, and remembering that protecting yourself is not the same thing as betraying the people you love.
