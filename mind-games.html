About the Book

What happens when human interaction becomes a game?

People do not always say exactly what they mean. Sometimes they hide behind charm, silence, guilt, anger, confusion, or carefully chosen words. At other times, they may not even recognise the patterns they repeatedly create.

Mind Games examines the psychological strategies people use to gain attention, avoid responsibility, control relationships, protect themselves, or maintain power over others.

Rather than reducing every difficult interaction to a simple label, the book looks beneath behaviour to explore the fears, needs, motives, and emotional habits that may be driving it.

Inside the Book

Key themes

01

Manipulation

How guilt, pressure, charm, silence, confusion, and emotional reactions can be used to influence other people.

02

Hidden Motives

Why behaviour often communicates needs and intentions that people cannot—or will not—express directly.

03

Emotional Patterns

How repeated roles and familiar relationship dynamics can become difficult to recognise and even harder to escape.

04

Power & Control

The subtle ways people seek control over conversations, relationships, choices, and emotional responses.

05

Boundaries

How clearer boundaries can interrupt unhealthy patterns without turning every conflict into a declaration of war.

06

Self-Awareness

Recognising not only what other people are doing, but also how our own fears and reactions may keep the game going.

For Curious Readers

Who should read it?

✓ Readers interested in psychology, relationships, and human behaviour.

✓ Anyone trying to understand confusing or emotionally exhausting interactions.

✓ People learning to recognise manipulation, mixed messages, and unhealthy relationship patterns.

✓ Readers who prefer honest reflection to empty motivational advice.

Why I wrote it

I wrote Mind Games because difficult behaviour is often discussed in extremes. People are quickly labelled, condemned, or excused, while the actual pattern remains unexplored.

I wanted to create something that helps readers recognise what may be happening beneath the surface—without pretending that understanding a behaviour means accepting it.
